CONSTRUCTION MARKETPLACE - FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS (FAQ)

Document: Platform FAQ
Version: 3.0
Last Updated: February 2024

---

Q1: How do I get started as a contractor on this platform?
A: To register as a contractor, follow these steps:
1. Create an account and complete your business profile.
2. Upload your contractor license, insurance certificates, and any relevant certifications.
3. Complete the identity verification process.
4. Submit three professional references.
5. Once approved (typically 3-5 business days), you can start bidding on projects.

Q2: What types of construction projects are supported on the platform?
A: The platform supports a wide range of construction projects including:
- Residential construction (new homes, additions, renovations)
- Commercial construction (offices, retail spaces, warehouses)
- Industrial construction (factories, plants, infrastructure)
- Specialty work (electrical, plumbing, HVAC, roofing)
- Civil construction (roads, bridges, utilities)

Q3: How are contractor bids evaluated?
A: Bids are evaluated based on multiple criteria:
- Price competitiveness (40% weight)
- Contractor rating and reviews (30% weight)
- Relevant experience and past projects (20% weight)
- Timeline commitment (10% weight)
Project owners can adjust these criteria based on project priorities.

Q4: What happens if a contractor abandons a project?
A: If a contractor abandons a project:
- The remaining escrowed funds are released to the project owner.
- The contractor's rating is severely penalized.
- The platform may suspend or permanently ban repeat offenders.
- The project owner may seek damages through the platform's dispute resolution system.
- A substitute contractor listing is expedited.

Q5: How does the platform handle material procurement?
A: The platform offers two options:
- Owner-furnished materials: The project owner purchases materials directly.
- Contractor-furnished materials: The contractor procures materials, included in the contract price.
For contractor-furnished materials, receipts and invoices must be submitted for transparency.

Q6: What are the platform fees?
A: Platform fees are structured as follows:
- Project owners: Free to post projects; 2.5% transaction fee on payments processed.
- Contractors: 5% service fee on earned project revenue.
- Premium subscriptions available for enhanced visibility and features.

Q7: How is project quality assured?
A: Quality assurance mechanisms include:
- Mandatory milestone inspections before payment release.
- Client review and rating system post-project completion.
- Third-party inspection services available for large projects.
- Dispute resolution team for quality complaints.

Q8: What insurance is required for projects?
A: Insurance requirements vary by project size:
- Projects under $50,000: General liability insurance ($500,000 minimum).
- Projects $50,000 - $500,000: General liability ($1,000,000) + workers' comp.
- Projects over $500,000: General liability ($2,000,000) + workers' comp + builder's risk insurance.
- Specialty projects may require additional coverage (e.g., professional liability).

Q9: How are construction delays handled on the platform?
A: The platform has a structured delay management process:
- Contractors must report anticipated delays 48 hours in advance.
- Delay reasons are classified as contractor-caused, client-caused, or force majeure.
- Contractor-caused delays may trigger liquidated damages per contract terms.
- Clients can approve timeline extensions through the platform dashboard.
- All delay communications must be documented in the platform's messaging system.

Q10: Can subcontractors be used on projects?
A: Yes, with conditions:
- Primary contractors may subcontract up to 40% of the work value.
- All subcontractors must also be verified on the platform.
- Primary contractor remains fully responsible for subcontractor work quality.
- Subcontractors must be disclosed to the project owner before engagement.

Q11: What is the process for project change orders?
A: Change orders follow a formal process:
1. Contractor identifies scope change and prepares change order request.
2. Change order includes description, cost impact, and timeline impact.
3. Client reviews and approves/rejects through the platform.
4. Approved change orders become addendums to the original contract.
5. Payment for change orders follows the same escrow and milestone process.

Q12: How does the rating and review system work?
A: Both project owners and contractors receive ratings:
- Ratings are on a 1-5 star scale.
- Reviews are posted after project completion.
- Reviews are verified (only parties involved in the project can review).
- Dispute resolution team reviews complaints of fraudulent reviews.
- Consistently low-rated contractors (below 3.0) face account review.

Q13: What environmental regulations must contractors comply with?
A: Contractors must comply with applicable environmental regulations:
- Clean Water Act requirements for stormwater management.
- Clean Air Act compliance for dust and emission controls.
- Hazardous waste disposal per EPA regulations.
- Local environmental impact requirements.
- LEED certification requirements if specified in the project.

Q14: How is worker safety enforced on projects?
A: Worker safety is enforced through:
- Mandatory OSHA compliance certification for all contractors.
- Safety plans required for projects over $100,000.
- Random safety audits by platform-certified inspectors.
- Incident reporting integrated into the platform.
- Contractors with poor safety records are subject to removal.
