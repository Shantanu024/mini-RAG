Version: 1.5
Last Updated: March 2024

SECTION A: CONCRETE SPECIFICATIONS

A1: General Concrete Requirements
- Minimum compressive strength: 3,000 PSI for residential, 4,000 PSI for commercial.
- Water-cement ratio: Maximum 0.45 for exposed surfaces, 0.50 for interior.
- Slump: 3-5 inches for standard pours, 4-7 inches for pumped concrete.
- Air entrainment: 5-7% for freeze-thaw environments.

A2: Concrete Curing Requirements
- Wet curing minimum 7 days for standard concrete.
- Temperature must remain above 50°F during curing.
- Curing compounds must meet ASTM C309 standards.
- High-strength concrete may require extended curing periods.

A3: Reinforcement Requirements
- Rebar grade: Grade 60 (minimum) for structural applications.
- Concrete cover: 3 inches for footings, 2 inches for slabs, 1.5 inches for columns.
- Lap splice length: Minimum 40 bar diameters for tension splices.

SECTION B: STRUCTURAL STEEL SPECIFICATIONS

B1: Steel Material Standards
- All structural steel must conform to ASTM A36 or A992.
- Bolts: ASTM A325 or A490 high-strength bolts for structural connections.
- Welding: AWS D1.1 Structural Welding Code compliance required.
- Surface preparation: SSPC-SP6 (commercial blast) minimum for painted steel.

B2: Steel Fabrication Requirements
- Shop drawings required for all structural steel prior to fabrication.
- Mill certifications must accompany all steel deliveries.
- Non-destructive testing (NDT) for critical welds in high-occupancy structures.

SECTION C: FOUNDATION SPECIFICATIONS

C1: Soil Investigation Requirements
- Geotechnical investigation required for all commercial projects.
- Soil bearing capacity must be determined before foundation design.
- Expansive soils require special foundation design (drilled piers, post-tensioned slabs).

C2: Foundation Types and Applications
Shallow Foundations:
- Spread footings: For well-bearing soil with capacity >1,500 psf.
- Mat foundations: For poor soil conditions or heavy loads.
- Minimum frost depth requirements vary by geographic location.

Deep Foundations:
- Driven piles: Steel H-piles, precast concrete piles for deep bearing layers.
- Drilled piers (caissons): For large load concentrations.
- Micro-piles: For limited access sites or retrofit applications.

SECTION D: MECHANICAL, ELECTRICAL, PLUMBING (MEP) SPECIFICATIONS

D1: HVAC Requirements
- Energy efficiency: Must meet ASHRAE 90.1 standards.
- Fresh air ventilation: Minimum 0.15 CFM per square foot per ASHRAE 62.1.
- Equipment access: Minimum 36-inch clearance for maintenance.
- Ductwork: Sheet metal per SMACNA standards; insulated to meet energy codes.

D2: Electrical Requirements
- Service entrance: Sized per NEC Article 230.
- Grounding: Per NEC Article 250.
- GFCI protection: All kitchen, bath, garage, outdoor, and basement receptacles.
- AFCI protection: All bedroom circuits in residential construction.
- Commercial lighting: Must meet ASHRAE 90.1 lighting power density limits.

D3: Plumbing Requirements
- Water supply: Minimum 45 PSI at fixtures, maximum 80 PSI.
- Hot water temperature: 120°F at fixtures for scalding prevention; 140°F at water heater.
- Drain, waste, vent: Per International Plumbing Code (IPC) or local equivalent.
- Backflow prevention: Required per local water authority standards.

SECTION E: ROOFING SPECIFICATIONS

E1: Sloped Roofing (>2:12 pitch)
- Asphalt shingles: Minimum 30-year architectural grade.
- Ice and water shield: Required at eaves, valleys, and penetrations.
- Underlayment: Synthetic minimum in all new construction.
- Metal roofing: Minimum 24-gauge; concealed fastener systems preferred.

E2: Low-Slope Roofing (<2:12 pitch)
- TPO membrane: Minimum 60-mil thickness; heat-welded seams.
- EPDM membrane: Minimum 60-mil; fully-adhered or ballasted.
- Modified bitumen: SBS or APP-modified; torch-down or cold-applied.
- Drainage: Maximum 1/4 inch per foot slope to drains.

E3: Roofing Warranties
- Manufacturer product warranty: 20-30 years depending on system.
- Workmanship warranty: Minimum 2 years required by platform.
- NDL (No Dollar Limit) warranties available for qualifying systems.

SECTION F: SUSTAINABILITY AND GREEN BUILDING

F1: Energy Efficiency Standards
- New construction: Must meet current IECC (International Energy Conservation Code).
- Commercial: ASHRAE 90.1 compliance required.
- Green building certifications accepted: LEED, ENERGY STAR, Green Globes.

F2: Sustainable Materials
- Recycled content: Encouraged for steel (>25% recycled), carpet (>10%), concrete aggregate.
- Regional materials: Preference for materials sourced within 500 miles.
- Low-VOC: Required for interior paints, adhesives, sealants, and flooring.
- Certified wood: FSC-certified lumber preferred for wood framing.

F3: Water Conservation
- Low-flow fixtures: Required in all new construction.
- Irrigation efficiency: Drip irrigation and smart controllers for landscaping.
- Rainwater harvesting: Permitted in most jurisdictions; check local regulations.
- Greywater reuse: Allowed for irrigation in states with enabling legislation.

---

SECTION G: CONSTRUCTION WASTE MANAGEMENT

G1: Waste Diversion Requirements
- Projects over $500,000: Minimum 50% construction waste diversion from landfill.
- Separate waste streams required: Wood, metal, drywall, concrete, cardboard.
- Hazardous waste (lead paint, asbestos, PCBs): Licensed disposal contractors required.

G2: Material Salvage
- Pre-demolition surveys recommended for salvage opportunities.
- Architectural salvage encouraged for doors, hardware, fixtures.
- Material exchanges and donations to Habitat for Humanity ReStores permitted.
