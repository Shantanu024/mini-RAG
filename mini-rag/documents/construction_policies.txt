SECTION 1: PROJECT INITIATION

1.1 Contractor Onboarding Requirements
All contractors must complete a verification process before listing services on the platform. This includes:
- Valid contractor license (state-issued)
- Proof of general liability insurance (minimum $1,000,000 per occurrence)
- Workers' compensation insurance for employees
- Background check completion within the last 2 years
- Minimum 3 verifiable references from prior projects

1.2 Project Listing Standards
Projects must include:
- Detailed scope of work description
- Estimated timeline with milestones
- Budget range with line-item breakdown
- Required permits and regulatory approvals
- Material specifications

SECTION 2: CONSTRUCTION PROJECT DELAYS

2.1 Common Causes of Project Delays
Construction project delays are one of the most significant challenges in the industry. The following factors are known to contribute to delays:

Weather-Related Delays:
- Extreme weather conditions such as heavy rainfall, flooding, snow, or extreme heat can halt construction activities.
- Seasonal weather patterns must be factored into project scheduling.
- Force majeure clauses protect contractors from penalties during natural disasters.

Supply Chain Issues:
- Shortage of construction materials (lumber, steel, concrete) can pause work.
- Global supply chain disruptions impact lead times for specialized equipment.
- Material price volatility can cause budget overruns leading to work stoppages.

Labor Shortages:
- Skilled labor shortages in trades such as electricians, plumbers, and welders.
- High turnover rates among construction workers.
- Seasonal labor availability fluctuations.

Design and Planning Issues:
- Incomplete or unclear architectural/engineering drawings.
- Last-minute design changes requested by clients.
- Conflicts between structural, MEP (Mechanical, Electrical, Plumbing) designs.

Permit and Regulatory Delays:
- Slow permit approval processes from municipal authorities.
- Unexpected code compliance issues discovered during inspections.
- Environmental compliance requirements and surveys.

Site Conditions:
- Unforeseen underground utilities or archaeological findings.
- Poor soil conditions requiring additional foundation work.
- Contaminated soil requiring remediation before construction.

2.2 Delay Mitigation Strategies
- Conduct thorough pre-construction site assessments.
- Build buffer time (10-15%) into project schedules.
- Establish backup suppliers for critical materials.
- Maintain clear communication protocols between all parties.
- Use project management software to track milestones.

2.3 Delay Penalty Clauses
Standard contracts on this platform include:
- Liquidated damages of $500-$2,000 per day for contractor-caused delays.
- No penalty for documented force majeure events.
- Client-caused delays entitle the contractor to time extensions and cost recovery.

SECTION 3: SAFETY STANDARDS

3.1 Mandatory Safety Requirements
All construction projects must comply with OSHA standards. Key requirements include:
- Personal Protective Equipment (PPE): Hard hats, safety vests, steel-toed boots are mandatory on all sites.
- Fall Protection: Required for work at heights above 6 feet.
- Scaffolding: Must be inspected daily and certified by a competent person.
- Electrical Safety: Lockout/Tagout (LOTO) procedures must be followed.
- Hazard Communication: All chemical hazards must be labeled and SDS (Safety Data Sheets) available.

3.2 Incident Reporting
- All injuries must be reported within 24 hours.
- OSHA recordable incidents require documentation and investigation.
- Near-miss events must be reported and reviewed.

3.3 Site Safety Inspections
- Weekly safety inspections are required.
- Third-party safety audits for projects exceeding $1 million.

SECTION 4: PAYMENT TERMS AND DISPUTES

4.1 Standard Payment Structure
The platform supports the following payment structures:
- Milestone-based payments (recommended for projects over $50,000)
- Percentage of completion payments
- Time and materials billing
- Fixed-price contracts

4.2 Payment Protection
- All payments are held in escrow until milestone approval.
- Disputes must be raised within 7 days of milestone completion.
- Platform mediation available for disputes under $10,000.
- Legal arbitration pathway for disputes exceeding $10,000.

4.3 Lien Rights
- Contractors retain right to file mechanic's liens for unpaid work.
- Platform requires lien waiver submission upon final payment.
- Sub-contractors must be paid within 7 days of general contractor receiving payment.

SECTION 5: QUALITY STANDARDS

5.1 Workmanship Standards
All work must meet:
- Local building codes and standards
- International Building Code (IBC) where applicable
- Manufacturer specifications for materials
- Industry best practices (ACI for concrete, AISC for steel)

5.2 Warranty Requirements
- Minimum 1-year workmanship warranty on all projects
- Material warranties pass through to the client
- Structural work carries a 10-year implied warranty under many state laws

5.3 Inspections and Sign-offs
- Rough-in inspections before concealing work
- Final inspections before project close-out
- Client walkthrough and punch list completion
